I currently work on generalization and approximation error of equivariant functions, the design and application of equivariant neural networks for AMO physics and robotics, and symmetry as a lens to understand uncertainty. Previously, I focused on computational imaging, with applications in gravitational lensing analysis.

I am advised by Professor Robin Walters with the Geometric Learning Lab as well as Professor Jacqueline McCleary with the Northeastern University Cosmology Group. I am also a visiting scientist with the AstroAI group at the Center for Astrophysics Harvard and Smithsonian with Cecilia Garraffo.

Here's some material that's been occupying my mind lately, snail-mail me your thoughts! Going forward, I'm interested in exploring methods of relaxed and approximate equivariance, characterizing distributional symmetry breaking, and applications of these ideas in molecular physics and materials science. If you are interested in collaborating on any of these topics, feel free to reach out.
